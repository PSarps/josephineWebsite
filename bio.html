Meet Josephine Ampomah Boateng

Hello! I'm Josephine Ampomah Boateng, an Optometrist with a Master’s degree in Health Systems Research. I'm passionate about providing accessible and quality eye care, particularly for underserved communities. Over the years, I've gained expertise in low vision care, pediatric eye health, and community-based rehabilitation.

My mission is to bridge the gap between clinical research and real-world applications for effective patient care. I’m constantly striving to make healthcare more accessible and impactful for all, especially in underserved areas.

Professional Background

I graduated from Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology (KNUST) with a Bachelor's degree in Optometry. Building on this foundation, I went on to complete my Master’s degree in Health Systems Research, with a focus on healthcare accessibility and the management of vision impairments.

Over the years, I have worked extensively on low vision care, particularly in areas where healthcare services are limited. My experience spans clinical assessments, research, training healthcare professionals, and advocating for the implementation of effective, accessible healthcare solutions.

Research & Projects

As part of my commitment to improving healthcare, I have co-authored 2 research papers published in scientific journals, focusing on the gap in low vision care in Ghana. I've also been a part of the HM-KNUST Low Vision Project, where I had the opportunity to train healthcare professionals and raise awareness on the impact of low vision care interventions.

One of my primary goals is to enhance the quality of eye care through advocacy and continuous research. I’m currently involved in several projects focused on creating more sustainable and accessible models of vision rehabilitation.

Skills & Expertise
  • Low Vision Care: Specialized in clinical assessments and rehabilitation strategies.
  • Community Outreach: Organizing training programs and raising awareness.
  • Research & Development: Focused on academic and clinical research, particularly in optometry and health systems.
  • Healthcare Advocacy: Advocating for policy changes and improvements in healthcare systems.
Passion & Vision

My passion lies in making quality healthcare accessible to all, particularly in regions where healthcare infrastructure is limited. Through my work, I aim to advocate for policy changes, create innovative solutions, and continue to promote lifelong learning in optometry and health systems research.

I strongly believe that early intervention and innovative healthcare models can drastically improve outcomes for patients, especially those in underserved communities.

Personal Interests

Outside of optometry, I enjoy reading, particularly articles and books related to the latest developments in healthcare. I also have a love for traveling, exploring new cultures, and discovering ways to integrate art with science. These activities keep me grounded and fuel my curiosity and creativity.

Let’s Connect!

I’m always excited to collaborate with organizations, research programs, or individuals who share a passion for improving healthcare accessibility and vision care.
